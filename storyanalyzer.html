Story Analyzer SMAD Capstone

Project Overview

In this project, I worked on as a front end developer on a team of eight people that included other front end developers, graphic designers, and back end developers. The objective for this project was to redesign a product called Story Analyzer, which analyzes text and creates data patterns and visualizations for users to analyze various documents. This product was originally developed by Professor Michel Mitri in the College of Business at JMU, which allowed us to work closely with the client and identify his needs. We used a Scrum methodolgy to deliver our project in sprints. Ultimately, our new design created an efficient and user friendly design along with fully functioning features on the back end.

The Process

We conducted this project in 3 sprints over the span of ten weeks. Sprint One focused on conducting user research, identifying our audience and creating user personas, creating the product's brand and style guide, establishing goals for the redesign and features we wanted to include, creating low and high fidelity mockups through Adobe XD, and beginning to build the new product through HTML and CSS. Sprint Two involved continuing to build the product, mostly through HTML and CSS, but also including some functionality as well. We also conducted more user testing after building our product up more to ensure usability. Sprint Three included implementing the functionality and adding data visualizations, polishing the front end design, fully implementing our brand into the product, and adding additional features to streamline Story Analyzer.
